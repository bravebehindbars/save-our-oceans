Chemical pollution

Chemical contamination, or nutrient pollution, is concerning for health, environmental, and economic reasons. This type of pollution occurs when human activities, notably the use of fertilizer on farms, lead to the runoff of chemicals into waterways that ultimately flow into the ocean.

Plastic pollusion

Marine plastic pollution is a type of marine pollution by plastics, ranging in size from large original material such as bottles and bags, down to microplastics formed from the fragmentation of plastic material. Marine debris is mainly discarded human rubbish which floats on, or is suspended in the ocean.

Oil pollusion

An oil spill is the release of a liquid petroleum hydrocarbon into the environment, especially the marine ecosystem, due to human activity, and is a form of pollution. The term is usually given to marine oil spills, where oil is released into the ocean or coastal waters, but spills may also occur on land.

How we can prevent plastic pollution

The simplest way to reduce single-use plastic is by opting for reusable products such as water bottles and carrier bags, reducing your individual plastic pollution footprint on a daily basis and ultimately making an impact on the amount of pollution reaching our coasts.

Ending oil pollution

By staying on top of maintenance and being careful and attentive during refueling, boaters can be a part of the solution. In areas where the oil industry has less of a presence, the small spill volumes from fishing and recreational boats can add up.

How to prevent chemical pollusion

Conserve Water. Use less water so excess runoff and wastewater will not flow into the ocean. Reduce Pollutants. Choose nontoxic chemicals and dispose of herbicides, pesticies, and cleaning products properly. Reduce Waste. Cut down on what you throw away.
